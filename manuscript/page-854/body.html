Chapter XXII.

Two Lives.

Only half of Moore's activity and resolution had been seen in his defence of the mill; he showed the other half (and a terrible half it was in the indefatigable, the relentless assiduity with which he pursued the leaders of the riot. The mob, the mere followers, he let alone.
